ANDES Technical Requirements Specification — ESO-published, architect flow-down
[R-AND-65]

The instrument shall have two observing modes: 1. Seeing-limited mode 2. IFU mode

[R-AND-65].3

The ANDES Software shall provide support to observing mode in Seeing limited and IFU mode configuration

[R-AND-65].3.SW2

The ANDES DRS software shall be able to reduce data for seeing-limited mode data and IFU mode data

[R-AND-70]

The baseline design of ANDES shall cover the wavelength range specified in [R-AND-94]. As a goal, the wavelength range will be extended to U-band and/or K-band.

[R-AND-70].1

The ANDES SW shall be able to support extension of U-band and K-band and tolerate gaps and wavelength extensions.

[R-AND-70].1.SW1

The DRS shall cover reduction procedure in the full wavelength range of ANDES and shall support the extension to U band and K band. The DRS shall be modular to tolerate gaps and further wavelength extensions.

[R-AND-88]

Spectral Resolution (TLR-A.1) The instrument shall provide an average spectral resolution of R=100,000 with a variation within [-10%,+30 %] across the wavelength range.

[R-AND-88].1

The ANDES SW shall not degradate the spectral resolution of the recored spectra more than [-5%; +5%] across the wavelength range

implied
[R-AND-88].1.SW1

The ANDES DRS shall not degradate the spectral resolution of the recored spectra more than [-5%; +5%] across the wavelength range

[R-AND-112]

Validity period of calibrations Except for standard stars, and scientific observations with simultaneous calibrations, night-time calibrations shall not be needed to fulfil the performance requirements defined in this document. All day-time calibrations shall be valid for at least 24 hours (goal: more than 48 hours).

also linked to SCAO work packages
[R-AND-112].1

The ANDES SW shall not require night-time calibrations to reduce the data and fullfil the requirements on accuracy, precisions, flux calibration and resolution. All day-time calibrations shall be valid for at least 24 hours (goal: more than 48 hours).

[R-AND-112].1.SW1

The ANDES DRS shall not require night-time calibrations to reduce the data and fullfil the requirements on accuracy, precisions, flux calibration and resolution. All day-time calibrations shall be valid for at least 24 hours (goal: more than 48 hours).

[R-AND-114]

Sky subtraction For observations in which optimal sky subtraction is requested (i.e. one sub-FoV is serving as a simultaneous sky probe), the sky subtraction shall be better than 1% of the measured sky background or the square root of the number of detected sky photons, whichever is the larger.

[R-AND-114].1

For observations in which optimal sky subtraction is requested (i.e. one sub-FoV is serving as a simultaneous sky probe), the sky subtraction shall be better than 1% of the measured sky background or the square root of the number of detected sky photons, whichever is the larger.

[R-AND-114].1.SW1

For observations in which optimal sky subtraction is requested (i.e. one sub-FoV is serving as a simultaneous sky probe), the sky subtraction obtained by the DRS shall be better than 1% of the measured sky background or the square root of the number of detected sky photons, whichever is the larger.

[R-AND-115]

Interpolation When interpolating onto a regular output grid, the data pipeline shall not increase the FWHM of a Nyquist sampled Gaussian by more than 20%.

[R-AND-115].1

The ANDES Data Reduction, when interpolating onto a regular output grid, shall not increase the FWHM of a Nyquist sampled Gaussian by more than 20%.

[R-AND-115].1.SW1

The ANDES Data Reduction, when interpolating onto a regular output grid, shall not increase the FWHM of a Nyquist sampled Gaussian by more than 20%.

[R-AND-119]

ANDES shall be fully integrated in the ELT data flow system. Deliverables are specified and described in AD8.

[R-AND-119].1

The ANDES software shall be fully integrated in the ELT data flow system. Deliverables are specified and described in AD8.

[R-AND-119].1.SW1

DRS data product shall be fully compliance with the ELT data flow system as specified in AD-8

[R-AND-120]

Data Reduction Software (DRS) The Pipeline shall be compliant with AD8. The data reduction pipeline shall provide science grade data products for both seeing-limited and IFU modes. The data products, from which the instrument and sky signature has been removed, shall be in physical units and information on signal, error and data quality shall be provided as a minimum. The software shall allow to combine multiple observations to increase the field of view or signal-to-noise.

Common Requirements for ELT Instruments — ESO standard, local flow-down
[R-INS-896]

A data reduction package supporting the real-time basic quality control, science-grade pipeline processing, and interactive off-line data reduction, as specified in #898, #1049 and #1050, respectively, shall be developed. All three functions shall be based on the Common Pipeline Library (CPL), HDRL, and Reflex, and support different use cases of the data reduction.

[R-INS-896].1

The ANDES data reduction package shall support the real-time basic quality control, science-grade pipeline processing, and interactive off-line data reduction, as specified in #898, #1049 and #1050, respectively, shall be developed. All three functions shall be based on the Common Pipeline Library (CPL), HDRL, and Reflex, and support different use cases of the data reduction.

[R-INS-896].1.SW1

The DRS shall provide algorithms in the reduction cascade to monitor basic quality control to be stored in the FITS header that can be processed during the night for quality control (e.g. RON value, efficiency from standard stars, etc)

[R-INS-896].1.SW2

The DRS shall provide science grade reduction off line interactively

[R-INS-896].1.SW3

All the DRS functionalites (offline reduction science grade, quick reduction for monitoring) shall be based on the Common Pipeline Library (CPL), HDRL, and Reflex, and support different use cases of the data reduction.

[R-INS-928]

Reduced data products produced by the instrument shall conform to the ESO DICD standard, including DFS keywords, and to the ESO Science Data Product Standard for the Phase 3 process as defined in AD8, section 3.1, AD9, and AD10.

applies directly
[R-INS-1049]

The implementation of data reduction Software for science-grade data reduction, generating data compliant with Science Data Product standard for Phase 3 process shall be based on CPL and HDRL as provided by ESO (according to AD8 section 4.7, RD2).

applies directly
[R-INS-1050]

The interactive data reduction system shall allow the data reduction to be adapted to specific data sets, and also to generate data compliant with Science Data Product standard for Phase 3 process. The implementation shall be based on CPL, HDRL and Reflex, as provided by ESO. (according to AD8 section 4.7, RD2).
